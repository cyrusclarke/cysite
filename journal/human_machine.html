Human or Machine

“I'm out of battery...”

I'm waiting for Gem to visit and handover the instructions that she said she would give me. In the meantime I’ve been doing some thinking about this line between technology, especially information technology, and us humans that has been so severely eroded. It is telling that many phrases that once belonged to machines have now been adopted by humans, creating a new cyborgian-like language.

The Cyborg Foundation asks its visitors if they are more likely to utter the phrase

“I'm out of battery” or “My Phone is out of battery”?

This led me to start thinking about what other situations could create similar human-technology hybrid phrases.

The first group take the form of the human fully embodying intelligent machines, speaking on behalf of technology through humano-centric techneologisms such as:


“I have no signal”


“I've run out of space”

“I got an upgrade”


With others, tech-stensions, certain activities and words have become so intrinsically linked with technology that their so-called 'everyday usage' has extended beyond the original use and indeed any usage immediately evokes notions of technology:


“I'm searching for it”

“I'm here”



The last group is also the strangest. These cyborgian ciphers occur where the human has clearly confused the role of information & communication technology related terms and applies them outside of their original context:


“I haven't got the bandwidth”



“I'm disconnecting...”



“Let's take it offline”


This is all happening in our reality, where we have in the main chosen not to pursue the embodiment of technology. It leads me to wonder how the vocabulary of the people of Cytopia might have shifted more dramatically to encapsulate the cyborgian world in which they live.
