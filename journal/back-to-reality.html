Back to Life

Today I woke up in reality.

I knew this immediately as when I turned to my left I found a smartphone in front of my eyes with a long list of unopened notifications that I immediately was repulsed by. I rushed to my desk and upon opening my laptop saw that my normal applications and services were back. My immediate thought was to start downloading everything I had learned in Cytopia.

While in Cytopia my smartphone had not travelled with me. I had realised that several hours in when I reached into my pocket to check the time and realised that my iPhone was no longer with me. It also took me a while to find a reliable internet access point which I eventually found in the local library. In all honesty the internet was not something that I actively sought out, I suppose I was preoccupied with interacting with the people of the new world.

Knowing that my time would be limited, I used this initial trip to Cytopia for reconnaissance, speaking to as many people as I could. I had only been gone what must have three days but I had experienced so much. This Cytopian world, though similar to our own, had a great number of differences. My time there was characterised by frenzied scribbling and sketching in notepads. My trusty camera was also on hand to shoot photos and videos.

The ideas that I came across have inspired me and completely changed my notions of reality. I have never felt so motivated to work to try and share these ideas through the means available to me. The momentum I felt this morning to immediately share everything I had experienced was quickly checked by the enormity of that challenge. I knew it would be close to impossible to make people understand in a straight-forward manner. Anything I divulge would likely be misconstrued as the talk of a crazy man and I am wary of this.

After some consideration, I have decided that I must split my focus into documenting research through this journal periodically, as well as exploring how I can use the technology of this reality to recreate some of the amazing upgrades I found in Cytopia. This will not be an easy task as the technology of this alternate reality has developed in a very different direction to that of our own, much like Gem had stated. What I had failed to understand was the power of this technology to shape entire systems, the way markets operate and ultimately people's behaviours.

It has by the same stroke made me much more appreciative of the good points of our reality. In many ways the experience has been a catalyst to help release some of the latent energy I have to advance thinking around ‘how things could be.’

After all Cytopia is just a case of what might have been.
